18. AIM(S) OF THE COURSE  
Develop an understanding and skills in life sciences as applied to agriculture, 
environment, and natural resource management. 
19. LEARNING  OUTCOMES: Upon  successful  completion  of  this  course, students 
should be able to: 
a. Develop  an  attitude  to  apply  the  scientific  approach  in  solving  biological 
problems. 

b. Describe how Mendelian ratios could be modified. 
c. Describe the classification system of plants into the different taxa. 
d. Use the classification system of plants to describe the different taxa of plants. 
 
20. TOPICS OF STUDY 
20.1. LECTURES 
a. Genetics 
• Mendelian Genetics 
• Population Genetics 
• Concept of Evolution 
• Evidence and Mechanisms of Evolution 
• Theories of the Origin of Life 
b. Botany 
• Plant Organs: Leaves, Stems, Roots  
• Morphology and Anatomy of Leaves, Stems, Roots, Flowers and Fruits 
• Plant Tissues: Epidermal Tissue, Ground Tissue, Vascular Tissue 
• Classification  of  Plants:  Vascular  and  Non-Vascular  Plants,  Plants  with  and 
without Seeds (Bryophytes, Seedless Plants, Gymnosperms and Angiosperms) 
• Monocot and Eudicot Plants 
• Tropism and Photoperiodism 
• Reproduction in plants (Plant Reproductive Strategies, Seed Development, 
Germination and Dormancy) 
• Phytochromes and Plant Hormones 
• Water  and  Nutrient  uptake;  and  role  of  nutrient  elements  (Essential 
Inorganic Nutrients; Macro- and Micro-nutrients) 
c. Ecology of Plants and Animals 
• Sampling Methods in Ecology 
• The Physical and Biotic Environment of Organisms  
• The Ecological Niches, Habitats, Communities, and Ecosystems  
• The Food Chains and Energy Pyramids  
• The Growth and Regulation of Populations 
• Agricultural and Non-agricultural Ecosystems 

20.2. PRACTICAL/LAB TOPICS: 
a. Seed Structure and Germination. 
b. Morphology of Seedlings of Monocotyledonous and Dicotyledonous Plants 
c. Roots of Monocotyledonous and Dicotyledonous Plants. 
d. Morphology of Stems. 
e. Morphology of Leaves. 
f. Morphology of Flowers. 
g. Mendel's Work in Genetics 
h. Mendel's Principles of Genetics 
i. Ecological Sampling
