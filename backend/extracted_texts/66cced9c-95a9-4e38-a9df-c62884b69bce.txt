•On average, full sibs have half of their genes

Genetic correlation of traits
•When two or more factors influence each 
other, they are said to be correlated. 
•The measure of this association is called the 
correlation coefficient. 
•Typical instances of observed correlation 
are:
•animals with high growth rates tend to be 
fatter
•animals with higher birth weights tend to 
have higher weaning weights
56

Causes of correlation of traits
•Pleitropy
•One gene influences more than one 
trait
•Linkage 
•Genes are linked (they are inherited 
together in most cases)
•The traits are affected by the same 
environmental conditions
57

Forms of correlation
•Genetic correlation, r
A
•This is a measure of the association between 
the breeding values of the two traits. 
•There is pleitropy if r
A
 0.
•Phenotypic correlation, r
P
•This measures the association between 
observed traits.
•Environmental correlation, r
E.
•This measures the association between 
environmental effects.

Parameters of association
•The strength and the direction of association of 
traits can be described by regression 
coefficient, b, or the correlation coefficient, r.
•r measures the degree to which traits are 
related to each other and is measured on a 
scale of -1 to +1. 
•The closer the r is to 0, the weaker the 
relationship between the traits. 
•The sign of r shows whether the association is 
positive or negative.
