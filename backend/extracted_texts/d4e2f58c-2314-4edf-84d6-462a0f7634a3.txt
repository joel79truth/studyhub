Genetic Correlation of the 
characteristics (traits) 
•When two or more factors influence each other, 
they are associated 
•And we say they are correlated
•The measure of this association is called 
correlation coefficient
•That is a measure of relationship between two 
factors
•The same applies to relationship between traits

Relationship between traits
•Easily observed relationships include
–Animals with higher growth rates tend to be 
fatter
–Animals with higher birth weight tend to have 
higher weaning weight
–Maize stalks with tall green stems tend to have 
big cobs

Relationship between traits occurs due 
to
•The gene is pleiotrophic
–Same gene influences two or more traits
•Genes for each trait are tightly linked
–They are located close together on a chromosome
•Environmental conditions affecting both traits

traits can be at Genetic and Environmental levels

Parameters of association
•The strength and the direction of association 
of traits can be described by regression 
coefficient, b, or the correlation coefficient, r.
•r measures the degree to which traits are 
related to each other and is measured on a 
scale of -1 to +1. 
•The closer the r is to 0, the weaker the 
relationship between the traits. 
•The sign of r shows whether the association is 
positive or negative.
