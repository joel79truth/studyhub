oPedogenesis is the term used to describe the formation and 
development of the soil profile.
oTo understand how soil is formed it is important to understand 
the nature of rocks and their  breakdown into soil parent 
material.
oThe minerals in rocks influence the resulting soil composition.
oHowever, other factors in nature also influence specific soil 
properties 
oTwo processes involved in the formation of soil are:
▪Formation of regolith by breaking down (weathering) of the bed 
rock.
▪The addition of organic matter through the decomposition of plant 
and animal tissues, and reorganization of these components by soil 
forming processes to form soil.
oThe process of soil formation is as follows: 
Rock ->Weathering ->Regolith ->Soil forming factors and processes ->True soil

mineral.
oThere are three main groups of rocks according to geologist.
oThe groups of rocks are as follows: 
▪Igneous rocks
▪Sedimentary rocks
▪Metamorphic rocks

Igneous rocks
oRocks from cooled magma (a hot mixture of elements).
oExtrusive igneous rocks: from magma that cools at the earths’ 
surface 
oIntrusive igneous rocks: from magma that cools below the earths 
surface.
oThe faster the rate of cooling the smaller the sizes of the mineral 
crystals. 
oThey are source of parent material for other rocks and ultimately 
for soils.
oThe examples are Granite, Syenite, Diorite, Gabbro, Dolerite and 
Basalt
▪Granite: consists of quartz, silica (S

▪Gabro and Basalt: dark coloured, with little or no quartz, consists of 
feldspars, hornblende and augite minerals.
NOTE: amount of silica determines the acidity of a rock

Sedimentary rocks (Stratified rocks)
oRocks from sediments (material that settles on the bottom 
of a liquid) 
oSediments are laid down by water or by wind, usually in the 
lakes, oceans or lowland regions and consolidate into rocks 
over time, and under the pressure of overlaying materials. 
oThree subgroups:
▪ Clastic sedimentary: rock fragments e.g. sandstone and shale
▪Chemical sedimentary: formed from chemical compounds settling 
out of water solutions e.g. limestone and dolomite
▪Organic sedimentary: formed from living or once-living material 
settling out of water e.g. coal

Metamorphic rocks
o“Metamorphic” from Greek means “change form”
oThey change form by heat and pressure within the earth. 
oCan be formed from igneous or sedimentary rocks e.g.
▪Granite into gneiss
▪Sandstone into quartzite
▪Shale into slate 
▪Limestone into marble 
oThe change can be due to:
▪Water known as Hydrometamorphism
▪Heat known as Thermometamorphism 
▪Pressure known as Dynamometamorphism

Weathering of rocks and minerals

Weathering 
o“A process of disintegration and decomposition of rocks and 
minerals which are brought about by physical agents and 
chemical processes, leading to the formation of regolith”
o“The process by which the earth’s crust or lithosphere is 
broken down by the activities of the atmosphere, with the aid 
of the hydrosphere and biosphere”
o“The process of transformation of solid rocks into parent 
material or regolith”
oParent material/ Regolith : unconsolidated residues of the 
weathering rock on the earth’s surface or above the solid 
rocks from which the soils are developed
Weathering 

Weathering 
oThree basic processes of Weathering:
oPhysical (or) mechanical weathering- Disintegration
oChemical weathering– Decomposition
oBiological weathering- Disintegration + Decomposition)

Biological weathering 
oThe weathering in which disintegration and decomposition of 
rocks take place due to biological agent (macro and micro 
organism + plants)
oBiological agents 
▪Man (Human being): Cutting of rock for building and construction 
bring out disintegration of rocks at greater intensity .
▪Rats, Rabbits, termites, ants, earth worms provide passage for entry 
of water 
▪Higher plants: Roots of higher plants enter through cracks and exert 
tremendous pressure which leads to widening of cracks and crevice 
(disintegration)
⁻Deep rooted plants provide passage for water entry due to growing of their 
roots deeper and deeper into soil .
⁻Water loving plants like mosses , lichens, grass, ferns continuously keep rocks 
moist which bring further decomposition of rocks .
