Soil texture
Mineral particles vary in size in 
the soil:
Boulders > 256 mm
Cobbles/stones: 64 – 256 mm
Gravel: 2 – 64 mm
Sand: 0.05 – 2 mm
Silt : 0.002 – 0.05 mm
Clay: < 0.002 mm
•Soil texture is the relative proportion or percentage of sand, silt, and clay particles in a given soil 
•Different soils have different textures due to differences in the relative percentages of sand, silt, 
and clay
•There are 12 soil textural classes

Methods of determining soil texture

Soil sedimentation test/Jar test
Procedure:
•Dry the sample in an oven for 24 hrs at a temperature of 105 °C
•Use sieve no. 10 (having openings of 2 mm) to remove gravel 
particles and other residues
•Collect the sieved soil sample and fill it into a measuring cylinder up 
to 30 mℓ
•Add water up to the mark of 100 mℓ
•Add 1 tea-spoon of dish soap/detergent
•Shake the mixture for 3 - 5 minutes
•Cover the top and keep it at a safe place for 24 hrs
•After 24 hrs, observe the soil layers (there is an expectation to see 
three (3) layers)
•Measure the depth of each distinct layer (the bottom layer 
represents sand, the middle layer represents silt, 
     and the top layer represents clay)
•Compute sand %, silt %, and clay %
•Using the soil textural triangle, determine the soil textural class

b) Soil Texture by Feel Method
The feel method involves the following steps:
•Wetting the soil sample by drops of 
water
•Checking if a ball is formed
•Checking if a ribbon is formed and 
observe its length
•Checking the grittiness and 
smoothness of the soil
