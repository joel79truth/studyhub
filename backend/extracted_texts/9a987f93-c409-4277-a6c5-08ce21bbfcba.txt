State any five assumptionsofthe kinetic theory ofgases
.
Here are five key assumptionsof the kinetic theory of gases:
1.
Gasesconsist of alarge number of tiny particles(atomsor molecules)that are in
constant, randommotion. These particlesare separated by distancesthat are large
compared to their ownsize.
2.
The particlesare considered point masseswith negligible volume compared to the
totalvolume of the container. The actualvolume occupied by the gasmolecules
themselvesisinsignificant relative to the space they move in.
3.
Collisionsbetweengasparticlesand with the wallsof the container are perfectly
elastic. Thismeansthat no kinetic energy islost during collisions- the totalkinetic
energy before and after collisionremainsconstant.
4.
There are no intermolecular forcesbetweenthe gasparticlesexcept during collisions.
The particlesdo not attract or repeleach other, and they move independently of one
another betweencollisions.
5.
The average kinetic energy of gasparticlesisdirectly proportionalto the absolute
temperatureof the gas. At agiventemperature, allgaseshave the same average kinetic
energy per molecule, regardlessof the type of gas.
These assumptionsformthe foundationfor deriving important gaslawsand relationships, such
asthe idealgasequationand the relationship betweenpressure, volume, and temperature.
