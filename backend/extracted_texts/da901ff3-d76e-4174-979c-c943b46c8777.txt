ISOZYMES AND ALLOZYMES
•  Isozymes are enzymes that perform the same function but 
which are coded for by genes located at different loci
•  Allozymes are variant forms of an enzyme that are coded by 
different alleles at the same locus.
•  Both allozymes and isozymes move at different speeds 
through gel because they differ in size and charge

RESTRICTION FRAGMENT LENGTH POLYMORPHISM
•  The power of this approach lies in the shift from examining 
derived phenotypes (isozymes) to direct analysis of the 
genotype
•  Discovery of restriction endonucleases, to allow direct 
comparisons of homologous DNA segments among individuals
•  Variations are monitored as changes in length of the 
defined DNA fragments produced by digestion of DNA with 
restriction endonucleases hence the term Restriction 
fragment length Polymorphism (RFLPs).

AMPLIFIED FRAGMENT LENGTH POLYMORPHISM (AFLPS)
Overview
•   AFLPs combines the power of RFLP with the flexibility of PCR-based 
technology by ligating primer-recognition sequence (adaptors) to the 
restricted DNA and selective PCR amplifications of restriction 
fragments using a limited set of primers. 
•Step 1. Genomic DNA digested with a pair of 
restriction enzymes which digest double 
stranded DNA to produce (Eco-Eco; Eco-
Mse; Mse-Mse)
•Step 2. Double stranded Eco and Mse 
adapters are ligated to restriction fragments.
•Step 3. Pre-selective amplification, a subset 
of fragments are amplified, using primers 
complementary to adapter sequence with the 
addition of one nucleotide (Eco+A and 
Mse+C)
•Step 4. The no of fragments further reduced 
by Selective amplification in which primers 
have an additional two selective bases

APPLICATION OF MOLECULAR MARKERS
1)    Characterizing mating system and paternity analysis
2)   Forensic science and criminology- detection of criminals.
3)   Genetic diversity analysis for conservation
4)   Varietal identification and clonal fidelity testing
5)   Assessment of genetic diversity & validation of genetic 
relationships.
6)   Marker - assisted selection- molecular  breeding
7)   Taxonomy and classification
8)   Studying phylogeny, evolution or taxonomy
9)   Identification of genes determining sex in dioecious 
plants, fish and other animals.
