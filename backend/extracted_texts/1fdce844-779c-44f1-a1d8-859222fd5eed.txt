The Lincoln Index calculation
The Lincoln Index uses the proportion of marked individuals recaptured 
in the second sample to estimate the total population size.
•The formula is given as: N = (M ×C)/R
•N =the estimated population size.
•M = the number of individuals initially marked and released.
•C =the total number of individuals captured in the second sample.
•R =the number of marked individuals recaptured in the second sample.

The Food Chains and Energy Pyramids
•An ecological pyramid is a graphical representation illustrating the 
distribution of organisms across different trophic levels in a food 
chain. 
•When food passes from primary producers to secondary consumers 
and then to tertiary consumers (carnivores),
•it is estimated that about 10% of energy is lost during the process
•Ecologists use the pyramid to graphically represent this and call it 
‘Ecological Pyramids’.
