Meaning of Risk
ÔāßRisk:defined as the chance (probability) of suffering a financial lossor
the chance (probability)that the return on an investment will be less
than the expected return.
ÔāßExamples:
ÔāßLoss of produce during storage, due to quality deterioration & theft
ÔāßLoss of produce in transit to markets
ÔāßFinancial losses due to changes in interest rate changes &/or currency
devaluation
ÔāßLoss of markets due to sudden changes in government policies

Difference Between Risk & Uncertainty
ÔÉėRiskimplies that an investor is aware of the probable negative
outcomes of an investment and its associated probabilities.
ÔÉėUncertaintyrefers to a scenario in which the likelihood of a particular
negative occurrence is unknown.
ÔÉėNonetheless, risk and uncertainty are used interchangeably

Market Integration/Linkages
ÔāßMarket Integration/Linkage:Refers to the expansion of firms by
consolidating additional marketing functions and activities under single
management.
ÔÉľIntegration shows relationship of firms in a market.
ÔÉľThe extent of integration influences conduct of firms and consequently their marketing
efficiency.
ÔāßIntegration is an attempt at organizing or coordinating marketing processes
to increase operational efficiency and acquire greater power over the selling
and/or buying process.

Market Integration: Horizontal Integration
ÔāßHorizontal Integration/Linkages
ÔÉľTakes place when a firm or agency gains control of other firms or agencies
performing similar marketing functions at the same level in the marketing
chain.
ÔÉľIn this integration, marketing agencies (e.g. producers) combine to form one
body with an aim of increasing their market power.
ÔāßWhen farmers form a Cooperative they increase negotiation power with buyers
ÔāßWhen farmers form co-operatives, they sell their produce in bulk and reduce
cost of marketing.
ÔÉľHorizontal integration of selling firms is generally not in the interest of

Market Integration...Vertical Integration
ÔāßVertical Integration/Linkage
ÔÉľOccurs when a firm combines and performs more than one activity in the
sequence of the marketing process.
ÔÉľLinking together of two or more functions in the marketing process within a
single firm or under a single ownership.
ÔÉľShortens marketing channel as it reduces number of middlemen.
ÔāßExample: A flour mill which engages in retailing activity as well.
ÔÉľExamples of Vertical Integration in Malawi:
ÔāßCentral  Poultry  Company:  Input  production-Poultry  production-processing-

Motives for Vertical Integration
ÔÉľMakes it possible for a business to exercise control over both quantity
and quality of product from point of production to point of
consumption.
ÔÉľRisk reduction through improved market co-ordination;
ÔÉľLeads to reduction of marketing costs (e.g. no more costs of hiring
distributors; no more costs for warehousing.
ÔÉľVertically integrated firms have greater market power than other firms,
in terms of sourcing supplies or distribution network.

Vertical Integration...Types
ÔĀĪForward Integration:
ÔÉľInvolves a firm assuming another marketing function which is
normally executed after an existing function of a firm
ÔÉľExample: A wholesaler assuming the function of retailing; a
producer assuming function of processing.
ÔĀĪBackward Integration:
ÔÉľInvolves a firm owning another marketing function which is
normally executed before an existing marketing function of the firm
ÔÉľExample: A Producer assuming function of supplying production
inputs; a processor assuming production function.

ÔĀĪCreation of WTO:Brought international trade rules to areas previously

ÔÉėStrengthened the dispute settlement mechanism.

94
WTO Rules/ Principles:
ÔĀĪNon-discrimination:Any trade concession that a country makes to one
member must be granted toallsignatories. The only exception is with free-
trade areas and customs unions.
ÔĀĪReciprocity:Any nation benefiting from a tariff reduction made by another
country must reciprocate by making similar tariff reductions itself.
ÔĀĪFair competition:If unfair barriers are erected against a particular country,
the WTO can sanction retaliatory action by that country.
ÔĀĪBinding tariffs:No country can raise existing tariffs without negotiating
with their trading partners.
ÔĀĪWTO has the power to impose sanctions on countries breaking trade
agreements.

Methods of Trade Restriction/Protectionism
ÔĀĪTariffs (custom duties):Tax on imports to restrict imports.
ÔĀĪQuotas:Limits on quantity of imported or exported products, e.g. maize.
ÔĀĪForex Controls: Include (limits on forex made available to importers; or to
citizens travelling abroad, or for investment; & high charges for purchasing
foreign currencies).
ÔĀĪImport Licensing:Requirements that importers must obtain licenses for
importation of products.
ÔĀĪEmbargoes:Involves government imposing restrictions/total bans on
certain imports/exports to/from certain countries.
ÔĀĪAdministrative Barriers:Custom delays and excessive paperwork imposed
on exporters/importers just to frustrate exporters/importers.
