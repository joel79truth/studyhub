18. AIM (S) OF THE COURSE 
The course aims at equipping students with knowledge and concepts in soil science in 
relation to crop production and environmental management 
19. LEARNING OUTCOMES  
Upon successful completion of this course, students should be able to:    
a. Explain the origin, classification, and distribution of soils and their relationship 
crop production and environmental management 
b. Describe the physical, chemical, and biological properties of soil 
c. Determine the chemical, physical, and biological properties of soil 
d. Understand the impact of soil properties on agriculture, natural resources, and 
the environment 
e. Evaluate   and   manage   soils with   emphasis   on   solving agricultural and 
environmental problems  
20. TOPICS OF STUDY 
19.1 LECTURE/THEORY 
a. Introduction to the soil 
• Definitions and concepts  
• Functions of soil 

• Principle components of soil and their interactions within the soil medium 
and the environment  
• Soil profile  
b. Soil classification and formation factors 
• The parent material  
• Climate  
• Organisms (Macro- and micro-organisms) 
• Topography (Relief) 
• Time  
c. The soil formation process 
• Classification and properties of rocks 
• Weathering of rocks and minerals 
• Physical processes and agents of disintegration  
• Chemical processes  
• Biological processes and agents 
  
d. Soil physical properties  
• Soil texture  
• Soil structure  
• Bulk and particle density  
• Soil compaction 
• Porosity  

• Soil colour  
e. Soil chemical properties  
• pH (Alkalinity and acidity) 
• Salinity 
• Electro conductivity 
• Nutrient concentrations 
• Cation/anion exchange capacity 
f. Soil biological properties  
• Soil organic matter 
• Macro and microorganisms  
g. Soil plant nutrient interactions  
• Nitrogen versus other nutrients 
• Phosphorus versus other nutrients  
• Potassium versus other nutrients 
• Sulphur, calcium and magnesium   
h. Soil conservation and management  
• Soil productivity and fertility  
• Fertilizers and pesticides  
• Soil drainage 
• Soil erosion and control 
• Soil acidity and salinity  
• Tillage systems  

• Soil analysis 
19.2 PRACTICALS 
• Examination of rocks and minerals 
• Soil profile description 
• Soil sampling  
• Soils Catena Concept 
• Determining soil properties 
• Field visits
