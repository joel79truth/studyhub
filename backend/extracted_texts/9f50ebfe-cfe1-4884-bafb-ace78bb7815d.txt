AGAR Seaweed 
(Red 
algae) 
β-D-galactopyranose 
and 3,6-anhydro-α-L-
galactopyranose, which 
alternate through 1→4 
and 1→3 linkages 
 Indigestible  
 Forms heat resistant gels 
 Emulsifying properties 
 Stabilizing activity.  
 
 Agar is added to sherbets (frozen desserts of fruit juice, 
sugar, water or milk) and ice creams 
 Retards bread staling 
 Provides the desired gel texture in poultry and meat 
canning.  
 Has a role in vegetarian diets (meat substitute products) 
and in desserts and pretreated instant cereal products. 
 
ALGINATES Brown 
algae 
β-D-mannuronic and α-
L-guluronic acids, joined 
by 1→4 linkages (ratio 
of the two sugars = 
mannuronic/guluronic 
acids) is 1.5) 
 Water soluble in the form of 
alkali, magnesium, ammonia or 
amine salts.  
 The viscosity of alginate solutions 
is influenced by molecular weight 
and the counter ion of the salt 
 Powerful thickening 
 Have stabilizing effects 
 Gel-forming agent. 
 Alginates are used in a variety of gel products (cold instant 
puddings, fruit gels, dessert gels, onion rings, imitation 
caviar) 
 They also stabilizes and improve the consistency of fillings 
for baked products, salad dressings and milk chocolates, 
and prevents formation of larger ice crystals in ice creams 
during storage. 
 They are used to stabilize fresh fruit juice and beer foam.
