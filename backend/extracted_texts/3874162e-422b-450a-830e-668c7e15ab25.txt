Environmental approach
•You improve animal performance through
•Good feeding
•Quality of feeds, frequency of feeding
•Improving animal health
–You prevent and control animals from 
diseases and parasites
•By vaccinations, antibiotics, sanitation, treatment, 
clean water

Environmental approach
•Through growth hormones
–That make animals grow bigger

Advantages of environmental approach

•e.g., change from bad to good

–Results get realised within a week

Problems with environmental approach
•Improvement tend to be temporal
–Once you withdraw the manipulation, 
performance goes down
•Such as withdrawing drugs, feeding
•The potential of an animal to produce 
is determined by its genetic make up
–If genetic potential of zebu is 320 kg, 
that is the maximum it can achieve, no 
matter what environmental manipulation

 Genetic approach to livestock 
improvement
•Goal is to improve animal performance by 
changing them genetically
•In general, animals play an important role on the 
livelihood and welfare of human beings
19

Genetic approach to animal 
improvement
•In order to change the limit of an animal, 
there is need to use the genetic 
approach
•You manipulate the genes or genetic 
makeup of an animal
•This elevates the genetic potential of an 
animal
•Thus, improve performance efficiency 
by exploiting the Genetic component
E+=GP
20

Genetic approach to animal 
improvement
•Goodness is that once you manipulate the 
genes, that tend to be permanent
•With this approach, the improvement is therefore, 
permanent
•You increase an animal potential by 
concentrating desirable genes
•Using various means that will be discussed in 
the course
21

Problems with genetic approach
•Despite having permanent gain, the 
improvement is very slow
•It may take four generations for the improvement 
project to be successful
•This may be > 15 years in Zebu Cattle
•It is shorter periods with smaller animals
•Due to high and fast reproductive rates and 
generation intervals
22

Problems with genetic approach
•Despite the permanent improvement, you also 
need to improve the environment so that 
improved genes could fit in and express their 
potential
•Hence simultaneously improve feeding, 
management, housing, disease control etc.

Problems of Genetic approach, Malawi 
case
•Most project in Malawi run for less than 4 – 5 
years
•And later get abandoned due to lack of 
personnel, funds, proper planning of breeding 
goals and programmes
•There is high labour turn over in universities 
and government research institutes
•Hence genetic progress does not seem to 
make progress
•Currently many NGOs promote genetic 
approach of crossbreeding in rural 
communities
–However, many lack capacity to implement with 
success

Animal breeding in brief
•Application of principles of quantitative 
genetic theory to improve animal 
performance genetically
•The aim is to improve traits of economic 
and cultural values
–That contributes towards a breeding or overall 
goal of optimal livestock production
29

Animal breeding answers three 
questions
•Where to go? ~ objectives
–e.g. to increase growth rate for local chickens
–To minimise inbreeding
–To conserve rare genotypes for local chickens
30

Animal breeding answers three 
questions
•How to get there? ~ strategies applying 
Quantitative genetics
–Should we go for selection or crossbreeding 
theories
–Which animals to breed
–How to mate the selected animals
Or
31

Animal breeding answers three 
questions
•Getting there ~ Implementation of the 
theory
–Setting up structures for breeding, evaluation, 
multiplication and dissemination
–Famer training
–Considering cross-cutting and sustainability issues
32

Animal breeding in summary
•Aims at genetic improvement of livestock by 
utilising the genetic variation available, both 
within and between breeds
–Remember, variation is a raw material for genetic 
improvement
•The improved performance leads to more 
products for the same amount of input 
resources
•Genetic improvement is gradual but most 
sustainable form of improvement of efficiency 
of a production system
