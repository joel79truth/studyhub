on Hand Tools and Implements 
Dominates the Agricultural Production 
System Especially the Smallholder 
Sector

FARM MECHANIZATION TERMS
❖Agricultural Mechanization; Involves Equipment from Manufacturing, 
Distribution, Operation, to Farming Process (land development to processing),
•Does not Necessarily Mean the Use of Big Machines for Farming Work, 
•Provides Sufficient Time Gap For Self – Adjustments,
❖The Main Power Sources Includes; Hand-tool Technology (Humans), 
Draught Animal Technology (Animal Powered) And Mechanical Power 
Technology (Tractors, Motors, Aircrafts). 

FARM MECHANIZATION TERMS
•Farm Mechanization – Technically Equivalent to Agricultural Mechanization,
•Involves Activities Occurring Inside the Boundaries of the Farm Unit, 

FARM MECHANIZATION TERMS
❖Tractorization;
•Application of Tractors, of any Size, to 
Activities Associated With Agriculture. 
❖Motorization;
•Application Mechanical Motors or Engines, 
Regardless of Energy Source, Agricultural 
Activities.

FARM MECHANIZATION TERMS
❖Selective Mechanization
•The Appropriate Choice of Size or Design of Tools, Implements or Machines 
for Specific Agricultural Situation or Farming Region Based on Technical, 
Economic, Social and Political Considerations,
•The Goal is to Optimize Efficiency, Reduce Costs, and Improve Productivity 
by Focusing Mechanization Efforts Where they will have the Most Significant 
Impact.

FARM MECHANIZATION TERMS
❖Appropriate Technology,
•The Type of Mechanization that is Best Suited for Introduction and Use in a 
Specific Development Situation,
•Appropriateness of Mechanization Input is Determined By the Technical, 
Economic, Social and Political Characteristics of Each Development Situation,

FARM MECHANIZATION TERMS
❖Agricultural Implement
•Devices Attached to, Pulled 
Behind, Pushed, or Otherwise 
Used With Human, Animal or 
Mechanical Power Source to Carry 
out an Agricultural Operation e.g. 
Tractor Mounted Plough, Hoe.

FARM MECHANIZATION TERMS
❖Agricultural Machine
•Mechanical Device That Has A Number Of 
Moving Parts Such As A Combination Seed 
Drill Powered By A Tractor. 
•Agricultural Machinery Is A General Term 
Used To Describe Tractors, Machines, and 
other Sophisticated Devices than a Hand Tool, 
Which are Animal or Mechanically Powered.

FARM MECHANIZATION TERMS
❖Agricultural Equipment
•Stationary Mechanical Devices Such as 
an Irrigation Pump Set. 
•May Also Be Used in Place of the Word 
‘Machine’ to Describe a Stationary 
Thresher or Grinder,
