Irrigation

Irrigation
•A method in which a controlled amount of 
water is supplied to plants at regular 
intervals for agriculture.
•Usually used in areas where rainfall is 
irregular or dry times or drought is 
expected.
•Types differ in how the water obtained from 
the source is distributed within the field. 
•Surface Irrigation, where water flows under 
gravity and is spread over the area. The 
water then infiltrates to the subsoil. Quite 
often, land gets flooded.

Irrigation cont’
•Localized Irrigation: Water is distributed 
through a network of pipes under low 
pressure such as Drip Irrigation-reduces 
losses due to runoff and evaporation. 
•Sprinkler Irrigation: water is distributed 
through overhead high pressure 
sprinklers. 
•Manual irrigation: Water is distributed 
across land through manual labor and 
watering cans. This system is very labor 
intensive.

Use of pesticides and herbicides

Pesticides and herbicides
•Pesticide: any substance or mixture of substances intended for 
preventing, destroying, repelling, or mitigating any pest
•Herbicide: an agent, usually chemical, for killing or inhibiting the 
growth of unwanted plants, such as residential or agricultural weeds 
and invasive species.
•The use of pesticides and herbicides has been on the rise.
•As many organochlorines became banned through the Montreal 
protocol, because they persist in the environment, they got replaced 
with short lived but highly toxic organophosphates.
•This is one of the reasons why some agricultural products of 
developing countries do not compete on world market

Burning of crop residues

Burning of crop residues
•Farmers remove crop residues for 
livestock fodder and for cooking fuel. 
•Others incorporate the residues 
during initial tillage operations. 
•Burning is becoming more popular as 
it shortens turn-around time between 
crops. 
•High cost of labor leads to burning
•High cost of farming tools increases 
the likelihood of burning.
•Burning also cuts the lifecycle of pests 
which might affects the next crop 
grown in the field.
