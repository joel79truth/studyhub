



1. Randomized Complete Design (RCD)
•Used when we are interested in comparing treatments (ie
there could be four treatments; t = 4) so that for each of the 
treatment we obtain a sample of observations.
•Concerned with the comparison of treatment means: μ1, 
μ2.......... μt (μ = mean)
•The objective of the experiment is to make inferences about 
the corresponding treatments.
•This design assumes that there is only one factor that can 
affect the results(not always true but will see later).
•Uses one-way ANOVA
•That factor is the treatment under study.

Advantages of CRD
•The design is completely easy to construct
•The design is easy to analyze even though the sample sizes 
might not be the same for each treatment.
•The design can be used for any number of treatments
Disadvantages of CRD
•Best suited for situation in which there are relatively few 
treatments, though can be used for any number of treatments
•The experimental units to which treatment are applied might be 
as homogenous as possible. Any extraneous sources of 
variability will tend to inflate the error term, making it more 
difficult to detect differences among the treatment means.

Comparing pairs of treatment means (separating 
means)
•We determine which means differ by testing the difference between 
all pairs of treatment means.
•So far we have achieve to find whether different factors have an 
effect on the response variable.
•We do not know which factor (out of many factors or treatments 
observed) has an effect. In many cases there may be only one factor 
that may be significant that the ANOVA table is telling but we do not 
know.
•Separation of means will identify which factor(s) is(are) significant.
•There are a lot of test you can use but for this class we are going to 
use only two; Tukey’s test, Least Significant Difference (LSD) 
test.
