3. Factorial design
•Involves the study of two or more factors.
•When you have multiple independent variables (factors) in a 
single study, it is called factorial design.
•Each trial or replicate of the experiment, all possible combinations 
of the levels of the factors are investigated.
•If there are a level of A and b levels of factors B, each replicate 
contains all ab treatment combinations.
•The effect of the factor is the change in response produced by a 
change in the level of the factor
•The change in response is frequently called the main effect 
because it refers to the primary factor of interest in the 
experiment.
