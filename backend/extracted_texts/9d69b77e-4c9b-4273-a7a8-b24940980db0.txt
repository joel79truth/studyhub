AIMS OF STUDY: 
1. To enable students understand and get familiarized with the mechanics and processes of soil erosion 
and methods of controlling soil 
erosion and conserving water for agricultural and domestic uses 
2. To acquaint students with the various techniques of estimating soil loss on agricultural catchments 
3. To acquaint students with the methods of evaluating soil and water conservation. 
LEARNING OUTCOMES: 

1. Analyse rainfall data and compute erodibility and erosivity indices 
2. Estimate soil loss on agricultural catchments. 
3. Comprehend composition of watershed and its effective management practices 
4. Design elementally soil and water conservation technologies for agricultural production 
5. Evaluate the performance of soil and water conservation techniques 
LECTURE TOPICS: 
1. Sources of water 
I. Hydrological cycle 
II. Precipitation and its measurements 
III. Sources of agricultural water 
IV. Rainwater Harvesting 
2. Characteristics of water resources 
I. Temporal and spatial variation 
II. Randomness occurrence 
III. Impacts of floods and droughts on crops, livestock, settlements and human health 
3. Soil Erosion 
I. Process of Soil erosion 
II. Types of soil erosion 
III. Quantification of factors of soil erosion (Erosion indices) 
IV. Effects of Soil erosion 
4. Predicting soil loss 
I. Empirical models for estimating soil loss 
II. The Universal Soil Loss Equation (USLE) 
III. The Soil Loss Estimation Model for Southern Africa (SLEMSA) 
5. Methods of conserving soil and water 
I. Biological soil and water conservation methods 
II. Physical soil and water conservation methods 
6. Design of soil and water conservation structures 
I. Design of a simple storm water drainage system 

II. Design and construction of bench terraces 
7. Watershed management 
I. Composition of watershed 
II. Catchment area conservation 
III. Arable land use and conservation 
IV. Wetland degradation and management 
PRACTICAL/LAB TOPICS: 
1. Analysis of rainfall data, computation of erodibility indices, and evaluation of peak surface runoff 
2. Field observations of soil erosion 
3. Estimation of soil loss using USLE and SLEMSA methods 
4. Design of soil and water conservation structures (storm water drain system and Bench terraces) 
5. Evaluation of soil and water conservation projects
