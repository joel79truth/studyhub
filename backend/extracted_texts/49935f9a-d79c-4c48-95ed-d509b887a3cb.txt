Mendelian Genetics
•Mendel’s monohybrid and dihybrid experiments gave the idea of 
dominance and recessive where the recessive gene is masked by the 
dominant gene and the recessive gene can be expressed only in 
homozygous state (dd).

oMendel’s law of segregation:-states that genes segregate during gamete formation 
and reunite randomly during fertilisation.
oMendel’s law of independent assortment:-states that inheritance of a gene pair on a 
chromosome is unaffected by inheritance of other gene pairs on the other 
chromosome.

Locus A suppresses the expression of the gene at locus B and it depends on whether there is a dominant 
or a recessive gene on locus B.
Gene at one locus alters the phenotypic expression of a different gene at a second locus i.e. Hair color in 
mammals:  mice
In mice, black (B) coat is dominant to brown coat color (b)
A second gene determines whether or not pigment will be deposited in the hair...dominant (C) for color 
results in deposition
If the mouse is cc, it will be albino regardless of the genotype at the black/brown locus.

Autosomal linkage
•There are two types, complete and partial autosomal linkages.
•Genes that are linked completely do not assort themselves independently 
as a result they are inherited in a block
•Partial linkages come as a result of crossing over at meiosis to make new 
combinations with other genes and are called recombinants
Modification of Mendelian ratios

•Autosomal linkage
–Frequency of cross over is used to estimate the distance between genes.
•Genes that are far apart have a higher probability of crossing over than 
genes that are close together.
•Genetics have a standard percentage combinations that 1% of 
recombinants means 1 map unit i.e. The distance between neighbouring 
genes. 1% = 1 cM(centimorgan)
