18. AIM(S) OF STUDY: 
To equip students with problem solving skills and understanding of the general principles 
of electricity, microscopic nature of matter and Thermal energy at a foundational level. 
19. LEARNING OUTCOMES 
Upon successful completion of this course, students should be able to:  
a. describe and demonstrate properties of waves 
b. use wave properties in solving optics problems and mechanics problems 
c. solve practical problems involving periodic motion 
d. solve problems related to fluids in nonequilibrium conditions.  
e. compare electric fields and magnetic fields 
f. describe the composition of matter 
g. explain the implications of the gas laws 
h. apply gas laws in solving problems involving heat 
i. demonstrate the gas laws 
j. use ray diagrams to predict and describe the image formation 
k. demonstrate properties of light 
l. explain the applications of properties of light 
m. describe nuclear structure 

n. predict the products of radioactive decay 
o. explain applications of radioactive decay 
p. explain how radiation detector work 
q. explain and demonstrate the relationship between electricity and magnetism 
20. TOPICS OF STUDY: 
a. Wave motion 
● Types of waves 
● Characteristics of waves 
● Properties of waves 
● Energy transfer by waves 
● Equations of a travelling wave 
● Standing waves 
b. Kinetic Theory of Matter 
● Statement of the kinetic theory of matter 
● Implications of the Kinetic Theory of matter  
● The Gas laws 
● Temperature scales 
● Kinetic theory of Gases (An ideal gas) 
● The Ideal gas equation 
● Van der Waals Equation 
●  Kinetic theory of Gases 
● Temperature and Internal Energy of agas 

● Implications of the Kinetic Theory of gases 
c. Fluid Mechanics II 
● Ideal Fluid 
● Equation of Continuity,  
● Bernoulli’s Theorem,  
● Torricelli’s Theorem 
d. Electricity 
● Coulomb’s Law  
● Electric charge and electric field 
● Electric force 
● Electric potential and Capacitance 
● Electric current,  
● Voltage (emf and pd),  
● potential divider 
● current divider 
● Ohm’s law,  
● resistance, (resistance colour code, resistivity), 
● circuit configurations 
● Kirchhoff’s circuit laws 
e. Electromagnetism 
● Magnetic Fields and Magnetic Dipoles 
● Magnetic Force 

● Magnetic Flux  
● Magnetic field due to an electric current, 
● The motor effect, 
● Electromagnetic Induction 
● Faradays laws of electromagnetic induction 
● Lenz’s law 
● Applications  of  electromagnetic  induction  (motor,  electric  generator,  electrical 
transformer) 
f. Physical Optics 
● Dual nature of light 
● Laws of reflection 
● Flat mirrors 
● Spherical mirrors 
● Applications of reflection (retroflectors, reflecting telescope) 
● Snell’s Law 
● Total internal reflection 
● Convex lenses 
● Concave lenses 
● Lenses in combination 
● Applications   of   refraction   (retroflectors   optical   fibers,   the   eye,   refracting 
telescopes and microscopes) 
g. Nuclear Physics 

● Nuclear Structure 
● Isotopes 
● Binding energy 
● Radioactive decay 
● Nuclear Reactions 
● Radiation detectors  
21. PRACTICALS/TUTORIALS/LAB TOPICS 
a. Measurement of g and effective mass of a spring 
b. Measurement of period of oscillation of a simple pendulum 
c. Demonstrating gas laws (Boyle’s Law, Charles’ Law and Gay-Lussac law) 
d. Measuring specific heat capacities at constant pressure and volume 
e. Measuring coefficient of viscosity for different fluids  
f. Demonstrating Magnetic field patterns and Electric field patterns 
g. Demonstrating the Venturi effect 
h. Using a Venturi meter to measure velocity of and flow rate of a fluid
