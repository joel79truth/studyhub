PRODUCTION ECONOMICS
пҒұIntroduction:
пғјThe widely accepted goal of agricultural economics is to increaseefficiencyin
agriculture.i.e. to produce more food, fodder, bio-fuel and fibre with minimal use
of resources.
пғјRequired agricultural outputs/products must be produced with the least amount
of scarce resources (inputs).
пғҳMaximum possible output must be obtained from a minimum level of given resources.
пғјProduction economics is the application of the principles of microeconomics in the
production of different goods & services.
пғҳThese principles includebehaviour of production costandrelationship between inputs and
outputs.
пғјProduction economics provides a framework for decision making at the level of a
firm for increasing efficiency, profits & sustainability.

SHORT-RUN & LONG-RUN PRODUCTION
пғјWhen discussing production, it is important to distinguish between
Short run and Long-run production
пғјWhen demand for a firmвҖҷs product changes, profitability of the firm
depends on:
пӮ§Ability of the firm to acquire more production resources/inputs, and
пӮ§Expansion of production capacity.
пғјThe firm can easily acquire some production inputs within a short
period of time,e.g. labour, raw materials and fuel.
пғјHowever, the firm may struggle to expand production machinery, some
equipment & buildings within a short period of time.

Short Run & Long Run
пҒұShort Run:
пғјA period too brief for a firm (e.g. a farm) to change its fixed resources (e.g.
machinery, equipment and buildings) yet long enough for the firm to acquire
variable resources, such as labour & raw materials.
пғјIn the short runsome inputs are fixed and some inputs are variable
пғҳe.g. A firm may be able to vary the quantity of labor, but cannot
adjust the amount of capital.
пҒұLong Run:
пғјA period long enough for a production entity (e.g. a farm) to adjust the
quantities of all production resources (both variable and fixed resources)
for production processes.
пғјIn the long runall inputs become variable.
пғҳe.g. the long run is the period in which a firm can adjust all inputs.
Intro. to Agricultural Economics135
Short-Run:
пғјSuppose demand for Chicken Feed at CP Feeds increases, from 2,000 tonnes
to 3,000 tonnes. To respond to this new demand CP Feeds can increase
production hours (by introducing a second production shift and employing
more labourers) and source extra raw materials within a short period of time.
However, the company can not quickly adjust the production capacity.
пғҳThis is Short Run adjustment.
Long-Run:
пғҳSuppose demand at CP Feeds (Ltd) keeps on increasing, there will reach a time
when capacity of the processing machinery and storage space for raw
materials will reach their maximum levels. The company would, thus, have to
expand the processing machinery and acquire more storage space. This
adjustment can, for example, take 2-5years be accomplished.
пғҳThis adjustment is a Long-run.

Law of Diminishing Marginal Returns (LDMR)
пҒұDefinition
пӮ§It states that as successive units of a variable resource (say, labour)
are added to a fixed resource (say, land), beyond some point the
extra or marginal product that can be attributed to each additional
unit of the variable resource will decline.
Or
пӮ§As additional units of a variable input are combined with a fixed
input, at some point the additional output (i.e., marginal product)
starts to diminish.
пҒұIt is also known as law of diminishing productivity

Market Structure: Monopoly 
пҒұA monopoly is a market in which a single seller dominates
пҒұThis market has one firm producing for the entire market a particular
good or service.
пҒұCharacteristics of a monopoly:
пғјThe single firm sets prices for its products at will (It is a price setter, not a price
taker).
пғјThere are many barriers to entry into the market for new firms
пғјThere is no or less variety of goods (supplies a unique product with no or few
close substitute)
пғјThe single firm has no or insignificant direct competitors.
пғјThe single firm has complete market power (i.e.it can alter the market price of
a good or service).
