Introduction
•Pectin is a structural heteropolysaccharide
contained in the primary cell walls of terrestrial 
plants
•It is a white, amorphous, colloidal carbohydrate of 
high molecular weight occurring in ripe fruits, 
especially in apples, currants, etc.
•Due to its properties It is used in fruit jellies, 
pharmaceuticals, and cosmetics

Occurrence
•It is produced commercially from peels of citrus fruits and 
from apple pomace. 
•20–40% in citrus fruit peel 
•10–20% in apple pomace.
•Extraction is achieved at pH 1.5–3 at 60–100 ◦C., in a 
controlled manner
•Liquid pectin product
•Powered pectin product

Gelling properties of pectin
•Gel a semi-rigid colloidal dispersion of a 
solid with a liquid or gas, as jelly, glue, etc.
•A pectin-gel can be formed when the 
ingredients are heated, thereby dissolving 
the pectin. 
•Upon cooling below gelling temperature, a 
gel starts to form. 

Gelling properties of pectin
•If gel formation is too strong,the extraction of the liquid 
component occurs and produce a granular texture. 
•Weak gelling leads to excessively soft gels.
