18. AIM(S) OF THE COURSE 
 The aim of the course is to introduce students to basic micro economic concepts 
and how they can be applied in the food industry. 
19. LEARNING OUTCOMES 
 Upon successful completion of this course, the students should be able to: 
a. Explain  the  basic  micro  economic  concepts  and techniques  used  in  economic 
theory. 
b. Apply these techniques in solving problems faced by the food industry 
20 TOPICS OF STUDY 
 20.1 LECTURES 
  a. Scope and Nature of economic theory 
b. Basic economic problems 
c. Theory of consumer behavior 
d. Nature of market structure (perfect competition, monopoly, oligopoly 
etc) 
e. Theory of demand 
f. Theory of supply 
g. Price determination in different market structures 
h. Price instability and stabilization mechanisms 
i. Theory of production 
• Factor-product model 
• Factor-factor model 
• Product-product model. 
j. Theory of costs  
• fixed and variable costs 
• break even analysis 

• economies of scale and diseconomies of scale 
 20.2 PRACTICALS 
  a. conduct a breakeven analysis 
b. Use  Factor-Product,  Factor-factor  and  Product – product  models  in 
solving problems in food industry 
c. Conduct product price determination in different market structures
