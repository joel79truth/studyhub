Sire component mostly used
•This is because the dam component carries

•While maternal effects are not genetic
•And tend to be pronounced on those with long

Sib analysis
•On average, full sibs have half of their genes 
in common
•Half sibs have one quarter of their genes in 
common
•We can use this fact to estimate h
2
by
•Estimating variation within sibs ~ within family
•Estimating variation between sibs ~ between 
families
•That is splitting variance component into 
between and within families
21

Sib analysis
•Variance between families = covariance within 
families
•Remember, covariance is a measure of similarity
•Due to common genes or common environment
•Variance within families = rest of variation not 
accounted for 
22

Sib analysis –use of ANOVA
•When we have balanced data
•That is equal number of offspring in 
each family
•Variance components can be 
estimated using ANOVA

Sib analysis, family structure
•In animal breeding we often use hierarchical 
family structures
•This is a nested design
•A sire is mated to a number of dams
•This results in half –sib families (that have 
their sire in common)
•Or full –sib families (that have both sires and 
dams in common)
