TOPIC 1
Planning techniques applied in construction 
works/irrigation projects
The principal objectives of  planning in construction 
works/irrigation projects are: 
•To achieve the most economical use of  labour, materials 
and equipment
•To obtain the best sequence of  the activities involved
•To decide the amount of  time needed for each activity
•To avoid hold-ups due to lack of  materials

Examplesofplanningtechniques
Withthedevelopmentofnewtechnologies,new
planningtechniquesarebeingdevelopedinorderto
makeprojectexecutioneasierandmanageable.These
techniquesinclude:
a)CriticalPathMethod(CPM)
b)UseofNodeDiagrams
(PrecedenceDiagrams)
c)UseofBarCharts

Critical Path Method (CPM) or Critical Path 
Analysis (CPA)
•Criticalpathmethod(CPM)isastep-by-stepproject
planning&managementtechniquethatdefines
criticalandnon-criticaltasks/activitieswhichare
presentedinanetworkdiagram.
•Thenetworkdiagramclearillustratestheflowof
theproject

Major steps involved  in the Critical Path 
Method:
Step 1: Specify and list all important activities required in 
the project 
(including the expected duration of  each activity)
Step 2: Establish a dependency table 
(this helps to have a clear idea on the sequence of  
activities ) 
Step 3: Draw the Network Diagram
Step 4: Estimate activity completion time. ...
Step 5: Identify the Critical Path 
(critical activities are identified)
