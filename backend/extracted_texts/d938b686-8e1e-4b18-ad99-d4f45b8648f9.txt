

Case Title: “The Tomato Farm Dilemma at Mitundu” 
Mr. Banda, a 36-year-old farmer from Mitundu in Lilongwe, owns a 3-hectare farm. He grows 
maize as a staple crop and tomatoes for cash. Recently, he has faced challenges: 
• High tomato spoilage because of lack of cold storage and delayed buyers. 
• Fluctuating tomato prices at Mitundu market: sometimes as high as MWK 1,800 per 
crate, but other times as low as MWK 700. 
• He is considering three options for his next production season: 
1. Expand tomato production on more land (high potential returns but risky due to 
market price drops and spoilage). 
2. Diversify into poultry (steady income, but requires new knowledge and start-up 
investment). 
3. Partner with a local cooperative that promises to bulk-produce and collectively 
market tomatoes (reduces spoilage, but requires trust and shared decision-
making). 
Mr. Banda must decide which option is the most beneficial for his farm and family. 
Required: 
1. Work in groups to analyze Mr. Banda’s situation using the Farm Management 
Decision-Making Process 
2. Show step by step 
3. Prepare a presentation and submit your work(handwritten) with names of your group 
members before Wednesday session.
