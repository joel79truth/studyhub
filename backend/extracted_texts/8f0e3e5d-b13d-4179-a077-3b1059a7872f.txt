18. AIM(S) OF MODULE  
To enable students to understand and apply hydrological analysis in the design of irrigation systems.  
19. LEARNING OUTCOMES  
 Upon successful completion of this course, students should be able to: 

a. Understand the hydrological cycle and apply the water budget model in irrigation system 
development  
b. Know the various types of precipitation 
c. Understand the different abstractions from precipitation 
d. Conduct stream flow measurement  
e. Apply hydrologic concepts in the design of irrigation and water engineering projects 
f. Understand the basic hydrologic steps that are applied when establishing an irrigation 
system  
 
  
20. TOPICS OF STUDY 
 
20.1 Lectures  
a. Introduction to hydrology  
• Hydrologic cycle  
• Water budget equation  
• World water balance  
• Hydrological applications in engineering  
• Sources of hydrological data  
b. Precipitation  
• Forms of precipitation  
• Annual rainfall in Malawi  
• Measurement of rainfall  
• Classification of rain gauges  
• Adequacy of rain gauge stations  
• Estimation of missing data  
• Presentation of rainfall data 
• Frequency of point rainfall  
• Plotting position  

c. Abstractions from precipitation  
• Evaporation  
• Interception  
• Depression storage  
d. Stream flow measurement  
• Measurement of stage  
• Area-velocity method of discharge measurement  
• Runoff  
• Hydrographs 
• Runoff volume  
e. Overview of water resource areas and units in Malawi  
• Basic hydrological steps in establishing an irrigation scheme 
- Irrigation scheme identification  
- Water source location  
- Identification of appropriate WRA or WRU  
- Identification of a gauging station in proximity to the water source 
f. Design flood estimation  
• Regional flood frequency method  
• Rational method 
 
20.2 PRACTICALS 
• Spatial analysis and catchment delineation in QGIS 
• Hydrological analysis using HEC-HMS  
• SWAT Model analysis  
• Determination of run off coefficients using landuse shapefiles in QGIS
