

 
Question 1 (20 Marks) 
You are a farm manager planning to grow 1 hectare of soya under rainfed 
conditions in Malawi. Your goal is to assess the profitability of this enterprise. The 
following information is available for your planning: 
• You will need 50 kg of soya seed at a cost of MK 2,000 per kg. 
• Land preparation requires 2 days of ploughing and harrowing, with labour 
costing MK 10,000 per day. 
• Fertilizer (NPK 23:21:0) will be applied at 200 kg per hectare, costing MK 
1,200 per kg. 
• Pre-emergence herbicide will be used at 2 litres per hectare at MK 3,500 
per litre. 
• Insecticide will be applied at 1 litre per hectare at MK 4,000 per litre. 
• Labour for planting, weeding, and harvesting totals 25 man-days at MK 
1,500 per day. 
• Expected yield from the hectare is 2,500 kg of soya, which can be sold at 
MK 600 per kg. 
• Assume overhead costs of MK 20,000 per hectare. 
Tasks: 
1. Using the information provided, prepare an enterprise budget for 1 hectare 
of soya. Your budget should include: 
o Total variable (operating) costs 
o Total revenue 
o Gross margin (Revenue – Variable Costs)/income above operating 
costs 
o Overhead costs 
o Net return to management (Revenue – Total Costs) 
2. Calculate the break-even price per kg of soya for your farm. 
3. Discuss two factors that could increase or decrease the profitability of 
the soya enterprise. 
 
 
 

Question 2: Enterprise Budget for a 100-Broiler Poultry Enterprise (20 
Marks) 
You are planning to raise 100 broiler chickens on your farm over a 6-week 
production cycle. Your aim is to determine whether this enterprise will be 
profitable. The following information is available: 
• Day-old chicks: 100 chicks at MK 1,500 per chick 
• Feed: Starter feed for the first 3 weeks: 50 kg at MK 2,500 per 25 kg bag 
• Grower feed for the last 3 weeks: 150 kg at MK 2,500 per 25 kg bag 
• Vaccination and medications: MK 15,000 total 
• Litter material: MK 5,000 for the cycle 
• Labour: 30 man-days at MK 1,500 per day 
• Electricity and water: MK 10,000 for the production cycle 
• Expected weight at sale: 2 kg per bird 
• Expected selling price: MK 2,500 per kg 
• Overhead costs (housing depreciation, equipment maintenance, etc.): MK 
20,000 per cycle 
Tasks: 
1. Prepare an enterprise budget for this 100-broiler enterprise. Your budget 
should include: 
o Total variable (operating) costs 
o Total revenue 
o Gross margin (Revenue – Variable Costs)/income above operating 
costs 
o Overhead costs 
o Net return to management (Revenue – Total Costs) 
2. Calculate the break-even price per kg of broiler. 
3. Briefly explain two factors that could increase or decrease profitability 
of the broiler enterprise.
